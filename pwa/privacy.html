Privacy Policy

Punkto · punkto.xyz
Last updated: April 2026

What Punkto does

Punkto is a location-based messaging app. You can drop short messages (atoms) tied to real-world 3D coordinates and see messages left by others at the same location.

Data we collect

  • Location — your device GPS coordinates are used to encode the Punkto address when you drop an atom. Coordinates are embedded in the atom and stored on the node server.
  • Atom content — the text and optional author name you enter when dropping an atom. This is stored on our node server and visible to anyone at the same location.
  • No account required — we do not collect email addresses, phone numbers, or any account information.
  • No tracking — we do not use analytics, advertising SDKs, or third-party trackers.

Data storage

Atoms are stored in an append-only log on our server at punkto.xyz. Atoms are public and can be read by anyone querying the node. Atoms are not attributed to device identifiers.

Location permission

The Android app requests Precise Location permission to encode your GPS coordinates into the Punkto address. Location is only accessed when the app is open. We do not request background location.

Third-party services

  • MapLibre / OpenFreeMap — map tiles are loaded from openfreemap.org. Their privacy policy applies to tile requests.
  • AWS terrain tiles — elevation tiles are loaded from AWS. No personal data is sent.
  • Google Play Services Location — used on Android to obtain device GPS coordinates.

Data deletion

Because atoms are stored in an append-only public log, individual atom deletion is not currently supported. Do not include personally identifiable information in atom messages.
